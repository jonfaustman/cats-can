What is Cats’ Can!?

Cats’ Can! is a supporting organization*, whose intended goal is to support Manhattan, Kan. (MHK) charities while supporting the Earth through recycling.

Aluminum cans are collected and recycled at the current return rate, with all proceeds going towards a local charity.

Local charities will be selected and will change each collection period (based on time and/or monetary amount.)

  • Cats can recycle!
  • Cats can support local charities!
  • Cats can change the world!

As I recently (and luckily briefly) lost my inside cat, the first charity that will receive our support will be the Riley County Humane Society (RCHS).

Why Cats’ Can!?

Recycling is important. Supporting local charities is important. Combining the two is simply logical.

How can you help Cats’ Can!?

Individuals

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

Businesses

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

If a new container for recycling would further the idea within your business, we can discuss a material donation. In other words, Cats’ Can! will purchase a container for you, with your committment to participate.

Charities

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

If you want to be added to our list of local charities, please contact us.

Hopefully you’ve noticed that helping Cats’ Can! is simple. You can also help by spreading the word.

You can also take cans into Howie’s Recycling & Trash Service yourself, just tell them they’re for Cats’ Can and they’ll add it to our tally.

P.S., if you need an information sheet to get your organization involved, try printing out this website—it makes a great one.

When can you help Cats’ Can!?

Right now! Well, maybe tomorrow – the point is to get involved soon. More people involved means more aluminum cans; more cans means more money for local charities; more money means more support.

Where is Cats’ Can!?

Currently Cats’ Can! is aiming to help local charities in or around Manhattan, Kan. Although there’s no reason the idea can’t spread further, in fact we’d love to see that – and are certainly willing to help. There are many aluminum cans to recycle, and charities that need support.

Who runs Cats’ Can!?

Cats’ Can! is the brainchild of Jon Faustman. Upon seeing an abundant amount of Tallgrass cans in one of Howie’s existing charity bins, collecting aluminum cans from local businesses for the benefit of local charities seemed like a foregone conclusion.
