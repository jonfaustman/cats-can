What is was Cats’ Can!?

Unfortunately, Cats’ Can! was not very successful at garnering enough traction and excitement in the community to continue moving forward. I highly encourage both recycling and supporting local charities; however, Cats’ Can! will no longer be active.

Cats’ Can! is was a supporting organization*, whose intended goal is was to support Manhattan, Kan. (MHK) charities while supporting the Earth through recycling.

Aluminum cans are collected and recycled at the current return rate, with all proceeds going towards a local charity.

Local charities will be selected and will change each collection period (based on time and/or monetary amount.)

How can you help Cats’ Can!?

Individuals

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

Businesses

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

If a new container for recycling would further the idea within your business, we can discuss a material donation. In other words, Cats’ Can! will purchase a container for you, with your committment to participate.

Charities

Simple! Save your aluminum cans, bring them to us, or arrange for a pickup.

If you want to be added to our list of local charities, please contact us.

Hopefully you’ve noticed that helping Cats’ Can! is was simple. You can also help by spreading the word.

You can also take cans into Howie’s Recycling & Trash Service yourself, just tell them they’re for Cats’ Can and they’ll add it to our tally.

Where is was Cats’ Can!?

Currently Cats’ Can! is was aiming to help local charities in or around Manhattan, Kan. Although there’s no reason the idea can’t spread further, in fact we’d love to see that – and are certainly willing to help. There are many aluminum cans to recycle, and charities that need support.

Who runs Cats’ Can!?

Cats’ Can! is was the brainchild of Jon Faustman. Upon seeing an abundant amount of Tallgrass cans in one of Howie’s existing charity bins, collecting aluminum cans from local businesses for the benefit of local charities seemed like a foregone conclusion.
